Winning in the MLB: Does $$$$$ matter?

A compilation of payrolls and win totals from the 2014, 2015 and 2016 MLB seasons.

By Owen McCue

It's all about the money, right? Year after year the Yankees, Red Sox, Dodgers, etc. toss money at top free agents and build the best team. But do they always have the best team? To find out, we tracke each team's total payroll and total wins from 2014-16.

Over the past three seasons, the Dodgers have spent the most money putting together their team -- $731,436,661 to be exact. They've also topped the MLB with 277 wins. In comparison, the Houston Astros have spent $184,518,474 and have won 240 games, which ranks No. 19 in the MLB over that span.

If you take a look at the scatter plot above, you'll start to notice some odd trends. For the most part, teams that spend more have produce more wins. Among the top ten spending teams in the MLB, only the Phillies finished among the bottom 10 teams in wins. However, the Pirates had one of the bottom five payrolls --$252,275,999-- in the MLB and finished in the top 10 in wins. Still as shown below when you compare the botom five teams in salary and the top five salaried teams, you'll see a drastic difference in wins.

At the same time, the Dodgers were the only team with a top five payroll that finished in the top five of wins. The Giants were the only other team with a top five payroll that finished in the top 10 in wins. It's interesting to see how a team like the Cardinals spent half of what the Dodgers did and have just one less win over a three-year stretch.
